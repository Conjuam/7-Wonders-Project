Statue Of Zeus At Olympia

The Statue of Zeus at Olympia was a giant seated figure, roughly 12.5 Metres tall, made by the Greek sculptor Phidias around 435 BC at the sanctuary of Olympia, Greece, and placed in the Temple of Zeus. Zeus was the sky and thunder god in ancient Greek religion, who ruled as king of the gods of Mount Olympus.

The statue was a sculpture of ivory plates and gold panels on a wooden framework. Zeus sat on a painted cedarwood throne with decorations of ebony, ivory, gold and precious stones. It was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. It was lost and destroyed around 5AD. Details of its form are known only from ancient Greek descriptions and representations on coins.
